Burger King Offers

The Burger King menu in India would be totally different from its offerings world over, however they decided not to change their most famous product – flame grilling their patties. Their best burger ‘The Big whopper’ couldn’t be served with the standard beef patty but in India their chicken and mutton patties would be flame grilled and offered to customers. In a country where religion defines food choices, Burger King realised that and worked on their vegetarian menu even before their launch. They decided to offer multiple reasons for vegetarian customers to also come back to them. They offered options such as Crispy Veg Burger, Paneer King Burger, Spicy Bean Royale, etc which took care of their criteria – crispy, spicy and juicy. Burger King in India weekly offers you can take advantage of to save money. For example, if you stuck for breakfast, an example offer you could get would be there King Egg Burger – 2 for ₹69.00. As well, some longer running Burger King offers include their Crispy Chicken Burger – 2 for ₹79.00 and their Crispy Veg Burger – 2 for ₹59.00. Lastly, Burger King has been long running partners with the Mumbai Indians and are always running offers with their cricket team.

Best Burgers On Menu

Of course, Burger King’s most famous and most delicious burger is their whopper. A standard on the Burger King menu is the Chicken Whopper, the Mutton Whopper, and the Vegetarian Whopper. In addition to their traditional whoppers, they also add in special edition whoppers. One of the new variations they’ve recently added in has been the Masala Whopper It’s the traditional whopper with a special Masala sauce and a few more extras thrown in. Now, the whopper burger may be a bit big for some people, so you can also choose from some smaller burgers on the menu. Some of these could include the Chicken Tandoor Grill Burger, the BK Grill Chicken Burger, and the Chicken Chilli Cheese Burger – all great additions to Burger King’s menu in India.

Burger King Menu Combos

They have the Whopper Combo Vegetarian and Non-Vegetarian, which includes the burger with their fries and a Pepsi. They also have special combo offers during particular festivals or sporting seasons. Their most popular burgers are Mutton Whopper, Chicken Whopper, Whopper Meal, Fried Cheese Burger, Crispy Chicken Burger, Onion Rings, Veg Chilli Cheese Melt, Crispy Veg Supreme and Crispy Veg Burger. Unlike the KFC menu or the Domino’s menu, the only type of combos available on the Burger King menu is getting a burger with fries and a drink. That’s because those are the only food items they serve. If they expanded their menu and included more varieties of food, you would find different types of combos available at Burger King.

About Burger King in India

Burger King is the second largest fast food hamburger chain in the world. It operates close to 13,000 restaurants in 100 countries. The Burger King menu is the original ‘Home of The Whopper’ and has been making burgers since 1954. In the year 2010 Burger King Corporation was purchased by 3G Capital. Everstone Group in India which managed restaurants such as Copper Chimney, Noodle Bar and Bombay Blue started talks with Burger Kings Holdings Inc. in 2013. The 2 companies decided to launch the brand in India as a joint venture. 3G capital wanted to expand since it took over in 2010 and Everstone could help get the right locations. Rajeev Varman, Burger King’s General Manager in North West Europe was made to head the India operations. He along with others at Evershine Group travelled the length and breadth of India to understand consumer taste and mentality. They realised they needed to make their Indian Burger King menu 70% Vegetarian and have crunchy and spicy options on their menu. Their first store opened in Select City Walk, in New Delhi in 2014 followed by a store in Mumbai in High Street Phoenix. Over the next year they decided to open 10 stores but only in Mumbai and Delhi. Burger King has a differentiated offering and decided to do a slow and steady ramp up. Burger King’s restaurants are available in most of the popular malls and food courts all around Bandalore, Kolkata, Ahmedabad, Chennai, Deli, and many more areas.
